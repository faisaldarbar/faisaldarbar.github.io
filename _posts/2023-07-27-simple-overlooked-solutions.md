### **Networking Issues**:

#### **Mobile Hotspot**:

##### **Cannot Connect to Mobile Hotspot on Windows 11? Try This Solution:**

If you're encountering issues with connecting to your Mobile Hotspot on Windows 11, here's an additional step that might help you resolve the problem.

1. Go to Settings by clicking on the Start menu and selecting "Settings."

2. In Settings, navigate to "Network and Internet."

3. Under "Network and Internet," click on "Advanced Network Settings."

4. In the "Advanced Network Settings" window, locate and click on "More Network Adapter Options."

5. You should see a list of network adapters. Identify the one that provides internet to your Mobile Hotspot, which is usually the Ethernet adapter.

6. Right-click on the adapter and choose "Properties."

7. In the "Properties" window, click on the "Sharing" tab.

8. Check the box that says "Allow other network users to connect through this computer's internet connection."

9. From the drop-down menu, select "Local Area Connection." This should be a Microsoft Wi-Fi Virtual Adapter responsible for enabling your computer to act as a Wi-Fi hotspot, allowing other devices to connect and access the internet.

10. Make sure **NOT** to select your Wi-Fi adapter, as it is the one responsible for connecting your Mobile Hotspot to the internet.

![ethernet-properties](images\ethernet-properties.png)

By following these steps, you should be able to fix the issue with your Mobile Hotspot. Once you've completed these settings, try connecting your other devices to the Wi-Fi hotspot again.

Hopefully, this simple addition to the standard solutions will help you establish a successful connection between your Windows 11 computer and your Mobile Hotspot. If you encounter any further difficulties, don't hesitate to seek additional assistance.
